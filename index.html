Dynamic Pricing
−40% OOS error

Offer Dynamic Pricing & Elasticity

XGBoost Elasticity Switchback CUPED

When conversion scores drifted wildly by city, riders saw the wrong price — and patches per market only hid the problem. I rebuilt how queue features enter the model so pricing stays calibrated across 5+ markets.

Problem

City-level calibration swung from +39pp to −14pp. Features that drove ~51% of model importance were skewed between training and serving, so local hotfixes never held.

What I built

  • A single representation fix for driver-queue features in the Offer Dynamic Pricing conversion model (XGBoost).
  • A city-gated hinge correction on the S2O elasticity engine, validated against live +20% / +50% experiment arms.
  • A discount-budget regularizer with per-city dual ascent after silent production defects hit 2–13% of traffic.

Impact

Out-of-sample elasticity error fell ~40% (≈5pp → ≈2.5pp) in gated cities, with rollouts protected by a 3-gate decision rule.

Search & Ranking
+14–20% cart

Learning-to-Rank & Semantic Retrieval

LambdaMART Two-tower FAISS Query NLP

Shoppers who can’t find products don’t convert. I owned Zepto’s search relevance path — from classic learning-to-rank to dense retrieval — so results stay useful under a hard latency budget.

Problem

Zero-result queries and weak ranking leaked GMV. Catalog search needed personalization and live availability without blowing p95 latency past 100ms.

What I built

  • LightGBM LambdaMART ranking with query understanding, then a two-tower neural retriever over the catalog.
  • FAISS ANN candidate generation plus a gradient-boosted re-ranker blending user, cohort, and stock signals.
  • Conversion-aware ranking that balances relevance with what can actually be fulfilled right now.

Impact

Search-to-cart rose 14–20%, zero-result queries fell 35%, NDCG@10 improved ~12%, and search-led GMV lifted 8–12%.

Fraud Detection
~40% £-recall

Acquiring Fraud Detection Platform

Risk Scoring XGBoost Tecton Ops Tradeoffs

Fraud teams don’t care about AUC — they care how much loss they recover at a fixed review headcount. I reframed Tide’s acquiring fraud stack around pounds recovered under a hard alert budget.

Problem

Millions of acquiring transactions each month, but only ~1,000 can be reviewed. Classic ranking metrics pushed the wrong cases to analysts and left high-£ fraud on the table.

What I built

  • A 28-rule engine paired with a calibrated XGBoost model under a unified 1,000 alerts/month cap.
  • ProductionAlertGate — budget → amount floor → model/rules — narrowing ~3M candidates to the review set.
  • 60+ point-in-time features, leakage fixes, time-series CV, and a 250→10,000 alerts/month ops tradeoff curve.

Impact

Captured ~40% of fraud £-value on out-of-time hold-out at flat ops load; isolated PAYLINK as 55% of chargeback £ in just 3% of volume.

Supply Lifecycle
86% faster TAT

Retriever — Driver Reactivation System

Lifecycle ML Uplift Ensembles Funnel

Inactive drivers are lost supply. I built Retriever to decide who to win back, how urgently, and with which intervention — then automated the workflow across a large share of India South Asia supply.

Problem

Reactivation was slow, manual, and uneven. Support volume stayed high while arrears and idle supply piled up.

What I built

  • Gradient-boosting + ensemble scoring to prioritize reactivation cohorts and automate outreach workflows.
  • Uplift and funnel analytics to lift First Trip conversion and activation efficiency.
  • Churn and re-engagement models to protect weekly active driver retention.

Impact

Rolled out to 35% of regional supply: 86% faster reactivation TAT, 16% fewer contact inflows, $350–500K annual arrears savings, +12% First Trip conversion.
